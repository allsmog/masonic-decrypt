How it works: The prover has a solved Sudoku but keeps it hidden. Each round, they randomly reassign the digits (1→A, 2→F, etc.).

Verification: The verifier picks any row, column, or 3x3 box. If all 9 symbols are present, that section is valid.

Zero-knowledge: The random permutation changes each round. Even after many verifications, the verifier can't reconstruct the solution!

Confidence: With 27 possible selections, each round gives ~3.7% more confidence. After ~20 rounds, confidence exceeds 99%.
